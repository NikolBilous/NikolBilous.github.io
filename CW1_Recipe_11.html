Roasted Pepper Soup

The procedure for preparing peppers for soup is not too long, and does not require effort at all, the more valuable this recipe is. The taste is interesting, typical for peppers, although here you can play with spices and sauces, then something completely unusual can turn out. Spicy lovers can use chili peppers in addition to the usual sweet, you can just add Tabasco at the very end, it will also turn out interesting.

So here's how to cook roasted peppers. The process can be memorized and used for more than just soup. If, for example, they are ready to be put in a jar and poured with fragrant oil, you will get a good appetizer or a component of future side dishes (for risotto and pasta - amazing). Place four large peppers on a baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil. Rub the oil lightly to coat the peppers on all sides. I love peppers and tomatoes covered with oil, they shine so beautifully.

Preheat the oven to 204 degrees. Here, special accuracy is not needed, we will determine by external signs.

Bake for 15 minutes (until obvious blackening appears), turn the peppers in the pan to the other side and another 15 minutes. You should get a little burnt and soft peppers.

Something like this should work for you. Immediately cover the baking sheet with foil and let it rest for 10 minutes. This steam room is needed so that the peel from the peppers can be easily removed. A very important point.

When the peppers have cooled, carefully cut them: remove the stem, remove the stones and, most importantly, remove the peel. The easiest way to do this is with a small vegetable knife. I just cut the pepper into quarters, cut out the seeds and peeled off the skin in one piece.

Let's prepare the roast. Finely chop the onion, celery stick and medium carrot. As a result, you should get three equal parts of the indicated ingredients. And a couple more cloves of garlic.

Pour a couple of tablespoons of olive oil into a saucepan and heat it up. First add onion and garlic. Fry for 3 minutes.

Then carrots and celery. I know that many people do not take celery very well, they say it smells strange and the taste is sharp. But wait, does the bow we are used to cause a different feeling? It's all about the preparation. But this frying “onion-carrot-celery” (sofrito in Italian) is generally almost a classic element of many sauces and pastas, for example, in my classic bolognese pasta.

After 3 minutes, add the peppers.

Followed by a teaspoon of cumin and a couple of bay leaves. Look carefully here, cumin spice is quite charismatic and not everyone likes it. If in doubt, or if the smell of her is alarming, do not add at all, so as not to spoil the soup.

Pour everything with chicken / mushroom broth so that the liquid slightly covers the vegetables. If there is no broth, and this often happens, pour boiling water, it's okay.

Boil the soup until the carrots are completely soft, it may take a minute or 5. Remove from heat (remove bay leaves) and puree with a blender. I always say that first puree as is, and then gradually (if necessary) add liquid. In other words, the first stage is the grinding of the components. You may be lucky and get a puree right away, but it often turns out to be a lumpy, thick mass. This is where the extra liquid helps, which helps the blender to finally grind the mass into a puree.

As I said, you can add hot sauce or any other, to your taste. Serve with crackers, a spoonful of sour cream or yogurt. The aroma and taste are very spicy, while the puree turns out unusual, and I think many will write down this recipe in notebooks.

Enjoy!
