Fake Risotto

Risotto is one of my favorite dishes. In any restaurant, if I doubt the choice, I order this particular dish. My blog started with it and at that time I, not understanding food at all, spent a lot of time to understand the essence of this dish, its ideology and philosophy. Risotto is amazing in its volume. This is always a gastronomic theater in your plate. There is no similar dish that clearly and quickly explains to even the most inexperienced eater what texture is. We always have a light crunch, masked by the creaminess of sauces with the addition of dense filling textures. The same is with taste, it is a multifaceted combination of aromas and aftertastes. It is never ordinary and everyone finds their own combinations that remain forever in the heart.

Start by preparing the shrimp. We take about 6-8 pieces for every 100 grams of pasta. As a rule, shrimp are already ready, they only need to be thawed. To do this, I simply pour boiling water into a cup and leave the shrimp in it for 5-7 minutes. Then we clean them up. If the shrimp are large enough, then you need to cut a vein. To do this, cut the back shallowly and take out the black strip. By the way, in this form, the shrimp opens beautifully with a “butterfly” during heat treatment.

Take an onion and finely chop it. It is better, of course, to take red onion, it is sweeter and more pleasant in pasta. But normal will do.

Cut 3-4 cloves of garlic into very thin slices.

Also take 6-9 olives. In this case, I had stuffed with lemon peels. You can use any olives or capers instead. In general, skip this step, or replace with fresh champignons.

Heat the olive oil in a frying pan and fry the garlic for a short time, literally a minute. This will absorb the flavor of the garlic.

Fry shrimp in oil. Since they are ready, we just need to stir the shrimp with butter for literally a minute. Thus, all the flavor of the oil (with garlic) will soak the shrimp a little. Then set the shrimp aside.

Fry the onion in the same oil. Until it becomes soft enough.

Pour RISONI-type paste (150 g) - these are small grains of rice. Sometimes they are called ORZO. Of course, you can replace it with another small pasta that you have at hand, but it is with risoni that you get the very tricky move with risotto. Usually, packs of such paste are quite small.

Fry the pasta in the same way as rice for risotto. Minutes 2-3. At this time, the oil soaked in the aromas of garlic, shrimp and onion will be absorbed into the paste.

Then pour in about 100-150 grams of dry white wine. This step is mandatory, it will give the right aroma and taste to the final dish. Let me remind you once again that due to the temperature, the alcohol will evaporate COMPLETELY, so this dish is safe for children and drivers. But the aromas and taste of good wine will remain in the pasta.

Stir the paste constantly. When the wine is gone completely, add the chicken (vegetable) broth. Little by little, so that there is almost no liquid when the pasta is ready. Taste the paste after each addition of water. When it is almost ready (al dente), remove it from the heat. It is important here not to overcook the pasta, the middle should still be crunchy in each "rice".

At the end, add shrimp and olives. Serve right away! Perfect with grated parmesan.

Enjoy!
